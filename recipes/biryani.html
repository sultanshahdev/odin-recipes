Recipe For Biryani

Biryani an Indian Food? Or Culture Itself

Biryani is an evergreen classic that really needs no introduction. India offers so much on its culinary platter but the one dish Indians unanimously love indulging in is the mouth-watering biryani. With local and hyperlocal variations having evolved into distinctive styles of biryanis, one is spoilt for options when it comes to experiencing this melting pot of flavours.

So if you are a die-hard fan of this delicious dish, take things up a notch and tease your taste buds a little more with the story of what makes biryani so extraordinary.

Ingredients

  • Basmati Rice
  • Meat/Vegetable
  • Yougurt
  • Garam Masala
  • Ginger Garlic Paste
  • Turmeric
  • Ghee Oil

Steps to make biryani!

  1. Take a bowl and soak the rice in water, while you prepare the rest.
    2. Slice the onions length wise
  2. Prepare garlic and ginger paste
  3. Cut the chicken into small pieces (as many pieces as you want) and mix it with yogurt.
  4. Take a pot with a round bottom and heat 4 tbsp of soybean oil over medium heat.
  5. Add chopped onions to the oil and fry them for 3-4 minutes but don't let the onions turn brown
  6. After 3-4 minutes add the garlic paste, ginger paste, salt, cumin powder and coriander powder. Mix them really well.
  7. After a minute or so add cinnamon, cardamom and bay leaf into the pot.
  8. Finally add the yogurt mixed chicken, spices and mix them very well.
  9. Wait until the chicken changes color into light brown (this will take around 5-6 minutes) and then add the milk.
  10. After the chicken is cooked it is time to add the rice.
  11. Add the rice to the chicken gravy, also add some water. While adding water keep in mind that the water level must be at least an inch higher than the rice level.
  12. Cover the pot and wait until the rice is cooked.
  13. Keep the pot in low flame and wait until the rice is cooked. Before taking the pot off from the cooker add green chilli and black peppers.
  14. Now serve the the biryani while hot.
